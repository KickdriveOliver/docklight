\par \pard\cf1\f0\fs20 VariousReceiveSequencesExample
\par Different ways to  recognize RX data as a "Receive Sequence" and trigger a response in Docklight. 
\par \cf0\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\ul\f0\fs20 |~~~~ DOCKLIGHT SEND SEQUENCE INDEX= 0    NAME=1
\par \cf0\ulnone\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\f0\fs20 Test data sequence: a simple "1":
\par \cf0\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\ul\f0\fs20 |~~~~ DOCKLIGHT SEND SEQUENCE INDEX= 1    NAME=HelloWorld
\par \cf0\ulnone\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\f0\fs20 Test data sequence: a full line of text 
\par \cf0\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\ul\f0\fs20 |~~~~ DOCKLIGHT RECEIVE SEQUENCE INDEX= 0    NAME=AnyCharacter
\par \cf0\ulnone\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\f0\fs20 Triggers after any RX byte received. For data streams with high data rates, this is not efficient and can easily overload your display and the Docklight processing. 
\par 
\par \pard\cf0\f1\fs19 
\par \cf1\ul\f0\fs20 |~~~~ DOCKLIGHT RECEIVE SEQUENCE INDEX= 1    NAME=AnyTelegramWithPause
\par \cf0\ulnone\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\f0\fs20 Simple way of grouping several RX bytes - we assume the end of a sequence/telegram/frame is marked by a minimal communication pause. Here a pause of 10 milliseconds is enough. The maximum data length in this example sequence is 100 bytes.
\par 
\par \pard\cf0\f1\fs19 
\par \cf1\ul\f0\fs20 |~~~~ DOCKLIGHT RECEIVE SEQUENCE INDEX= 2    NAME=AnyLineWithCR
\par \cf0\ulnone\f1\fs19 
\par \pard\fs19 
\par \cf1\f0\fs20 Typical text-based telegrams/frames are ended with a CR / decimal 13 byte. This Receive Sequence will capture RX data as a full line of text, up to a length of 80 characters.
\par
